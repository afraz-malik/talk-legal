  2. Confidentiality

    • Confidential Information shall not include information that is generally available to and known by the public at the time of disclosure to the Recipient, provided that such disclosure is through no fault, directly or indirectly, of the Recipient.
    • Permitted disclosures. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to prevent disclosure of the Confidential Information as may be required by applicable law or regulation, or pursuant to the valid order of a court or an authorized government agency, provided that the disclosure does not exceed the extent of disclosure required by such law, regulation, or order. In any case, the Recipient, or its Representative, shall promptly provide written notice to the Disclosing Party of any such law, regulation, or order.

The Recipient understands and acknowledges that its obligations under this Agreement with regard to any particular Confidential Information shall commence immediately upon the Effective Date between the Parties or the Recipient first having access to such Confidential Information, whichever comes first. Such period shall continue either (i) for three (3) years after expiration or termination of this Agreement or (ii) until such Confidential Information has become public knowledge other than as a result of Recipient’s breach of this Agreement.

  4. Degree of Care
    • The Recipient shall not use Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party for any purpose other than for the Purpose and shall hold in strictest confidence such Confidential Information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Recipient under this Agreement. The Recipient shall protect and safeguard the confidentiality of all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party with at least the same degree of care as the Recipient would in protecting its own confidential information, but in no event with less than a commercially reasonable degree of care, including but not limited to:
      • preventing any disclosure, publication, communication, or otherwise making available Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, or allow it to be disclosed, published, communicated, or made available, in whole or part, to any person whatsoever, not having a need to know or authority to know;
      • using any Confidential Information, except as may be required in connection with the Purpose, or if authorized in writing by the Disclosing Party or its authorized Representative;
      • preventing any access by a third-party, not having a need to know or authority to know; and
      • preventing the copying of any documents, records, files, media, or other resources that may contain Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party.
    • The Recipient shall not disclose any Confidential Information to any person other than to its Representatives that have a need to know such information in connection with the Purpose and that such Representatives shall be informed of this Agreement and shall be directed to maintain the confidentiality of such Confidential Information.
  5. No License.
    • Unless otherwise agreed to in writing by the Disclosing Party or its Representatives, Confidential Information disclosed hereunder shall remain the property of the Disclosing Party. Disclosure of Confidential Information to the Disclosing Party shall not constitute any grant, option, or license to the Recipient, under any patent, trade secret, or other rights now or hereinafter held by the Disclosing Party, unless expressly agreed to in writing by both Parties or their respective Representatives.

  7. Remedies
    • The Recipient understands and acknowledges that disclosure or improper usage of the Confidential Information by the Recipient may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party. The Disclosing Party's Confidential Information and its ability to reserve it for the exclusive knowledge and use of the Disclosing Party is of great competitive importance and commercial value to the Disclosing Party, in which remedies at law may not be adequate. In the event of a breach or threatened breach by the Recipient of this Agreement, the Recipient agrees that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek, in addition to other potential and/or available remedies, a temporary or permanent injunction or other equitable relief against such breach or threatened breach from any court of competent jurisdiction, without the necessity of showing any actual damages or that monetary damages would not afford an adequate remedy. The aforementioned equitable relief shall not be in lieu of legal remedies, monetary damages, or other available forms of damages or relief.
