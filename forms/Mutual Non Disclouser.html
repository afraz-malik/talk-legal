Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

Business Relations

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement"), dated as of ________ (" Effective Date"), is agreed by and between [COMPANY 1], with primary offices located at [ADDRESS] (“ Company 1”) , and [COMPANY 2] , with primary offices located at [ADDRESS] (“ Company 2”) .

Company 1 and Company 2 are individually referred to herein as, the “Party”, and collectively as, the “Parties”.

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to explore and engage in discussions regarding a potential business opportunity of mutual interest between the Parties (“Purpose”); and

WHEREAS, in pursuit of such Purpose, either Party may disclose (a “Disclosing Party ”) to the other Party (a “Recipient”) confidential information of such Party.

NOW, THEREFORE,for valuable consideration of the rights and obligations hereunder, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

  1. Confidentiality
    • The Parties understand and acknowledge that in connection with the Purpose, each Party, and its Representatives, will receive, have access to, and learn confidential, secret, and proprietary information, documents, materials, data, and other information, in tangible and intangible form, of and relating to the other Party and its Affiliates, such as any information not generally known to the public, whether oral, in writing, electronic means, or any other form or medium, relating directly or indirectly to business processes and practices, plans, documents, research, operations, services, strategies, techniques, agreements, contracts, terms of agreements, transactions, potential transactions, compilations, metadata, technologies, know-how, trade secrets, computer software, applications, software design, web design, work-in-process, databases, device configurations, manuals, records, articles, systems, material, sources of material, supplier information, vendor information, financial information, legal information, marketing information, advertising information, credit information, design information, staffing information, developments, reports, internal controls, graphics, market studies, sales information, revenue, costs, notes, communications, algorithms, product plans, designs, styles, models, ideas, audiovisual programs, intellectual property such as copyrights, trademarks, patents, inventions, unpublished patent applications, and original works of authorship, discoveries, vendor and/or customer lists, client information, and manufacturing information, and distributor lists (" Confidential Information").The Parties understand that this list may not be exhaustive, and that Confidential Information of the other Party also includes other information that is marked as, or can be reasonably interpreted and identified as, confidential.
    • The Parties further understand and acknowledge that this Confidential Information and the Disclosing Party's ability to reserve it for the exclusive knowledge and use of the Disclosing Party is of great competitive importance and commercial value to the Disclosing Party, and that improper use or disclosure of the Confidential Information by the Recipient might cause the Disclosing Party to incur financial costs or loss of business advantage, in which case Recipient may be subject to civil damages or criminal penalties
    • Confidential Information shall not include information that is generally available to and known by the public at the time of disclosure to the Recipient, provided that such disclosure is through no fault, directly or indirectly, of the Recipient.
    • Confidential Information shall not include information that is generally available to and known by the public at the time of disclosure to a Recipient, provided that such disclosure is through no direct or indirect fault of a Recipient.
    • Permitted disclosures. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to prevent disclosure of Confidential Information as may be required by applicable law or regulation, or pursuant to the valid order of a court or an authorized government agency, provided that the disclosure does not exceed the extent of disclosure required by such law, regulation, or order. In any case, the Recipient, or its Representative, shall promptly provide written notice to the Disclosing Party of any such law, regulation, or order
    • Period of Confidentiality Obligations.Each Party understands and acknowledges that its obligations under this Agreement with regard to any particular Confidential Information shall commence immediately upon the Effective Date between the Parties or such Party first having access to such Confidential Information, whichever comes first. Such period shall continue either (i) for three (3) years after expiration or termination of this Agreement or (ii) until such Confidential Information has become public knowledge other than as a result a Party’s breach of this Agreement.
  2. Degree of Care
    • The Parties shall not use Confidential Information of the other Party for any purpose other than for the Purpose and shall hold in strictest confidence such Confidential Information disclosed by Disclosing Party to the Recipient under this Agreement. The Recipient shall protect and safeguard the confidentiality of all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party with at least the same degree of care as the Recipient would in protecting its own Confidential Information, but in no event with less than a commercially reasonable degree of care, including but not limited to:
      • preventing any disclosure, publication, communication, or otherwise making available Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, or allow it to be disclosed, published, communicated, or made available, in whole or part, to any person whatsoever, not having a need to know or authority to know;
      • using any Confidential Information, except as may be required in connection with the Purpose or authorized in writing by the Disclosing Party, or its authorized Representative;
      • preventing any access by a third-party, not having a need to know or authority to know; and
      • preventing the copying of any documents, records, files, media, or other resources that may contain Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party
    • The Recipient shall not disclose any Confidential Information to any person other than to its Affiliates, employees, agents, representatives (including attorneys, accountants, consultants and advisors), directors, officers, employees and employees of Affiliates (collectively, “ Representatives”) that have a need to know such information in connection with the Purpose and that such Representatives shall be informed of this Agreement and shall be directed to maintain the confidentiality of such Confidential Information. “Affiliate” shall mean any corporation, company, partnership, joint venture or other entity which controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party hereto; for purposes of this definition, “control” shall mean (a) in the case of corporate entities, direct or indirect ownership of at least fifty percent (50%) of the stock or shares having the right to vote for the election of directors, and (b) in the case of non-corporate entities, direct or indirect ownership of at least fifty percent (50%) of the equity interest with the power to direct the management and policies of such non-corporate entities.
  3. No License.
    • Unless otherwise agreed to in writing by the Disclosing Party or its Representatives, Confidential Information disclosed hereunder shall remain the property of the Disclosing Party. Disclosure of Confidential Information to the Disclosing Party shall not constitute any grant, option, or license to the Recipient, under any patent, trade secret, or other rights now or hereinafter held by the Disclosing Party, unless expressly agreed to in writing by both Parties, or their respective Representatives.
  4. Return or Destruction of Confidential Information
    • At any time during or after the term of this Agreement and at the Disclosing Party's written request, or after expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Recipient and shall either destroy all copies all copies, whether in written, electronic, or other form or media, of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information and certify in writing to the Disclosing Party that such Confidential Information has been destroyed, or upon Disclosing Party’s request, return all such copies to the Disclosing Party instead
  5. Remedies
    • Each Party understands and acknowledges that disclosure or improper usage of the Confidential Information by the Recipient may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party, and that the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information and its ability to reserve it for the exclusive knowledge and use of the Disclosing Party is of great competitive importance and commercial value to the Disclosing Party, in which remedies at law may not be adequate. In the event of a breach or threatened breach by the Recipient of any of the provisions of this Agreement, such Recipient consents that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek, in addition to other potential and/or available remedies, a temporary or permanent injunction or other equitable relief against such breach or threatened breach from any court of competent jurisdiction, without the necessity of showing any actual damages or that monetary damages would not afford an adequate remedy. The aforementioned equitable relief shall not be in lieu of legal remedies, monetary damages, or other available forms of damages or relief.
  6. No Assignment
    • No Party may assign this Agreement or any part hereof. Any purported assignment by either Party shall be null and void from the initial date of purported assignment.
  7. Governing Law, Jurisdiction, and Venue
    • This Agreement, for all purposes, shall be construed in accordance with laws of the State of [____] without regard to conflicts-of-law principles. Any action or proceeding by either Party to enforce this Agreement shall be brought only in any state or federal court located in the State of [______].
  8. Entire Agreement
    • Unless specifically provided herein, this Agreement contains all the understandings and representations between the Parties pertaining to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous understandings, agreements, representations, and warranties, both written and oral, with respect to such subject matter.
  9. Modification and Waiver
    • No modification of or amendment to this Agreement, nor any waiver of any rights under this Agreement, will be effective unless in a writing signed by the Parties or an authorized Representative. Waiver by either Party of a breach of any provision of this Agreement will not operate as a waiver of any other or subsequent breach
  10. Severability
    • Should any provision of this Agreement be held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be enforceable only if modified, or if any portion of this Agreement shall be held unenforceable and stricken, such holding shall not affect the validity of the remainder of this Agreement, the balance of which shall continue to be binding upon the Parties with any such modification to become a part hereof and treated as though originally set forth in this Agreement.
  11. Counterparts
    • This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which taken together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Delivery of an executed counterpart's signature page of this Agreement, by facsimile, email in portable document format .pdf, or by any other electronic means, such as DocuSign, that is intended to preserve the original graphic appearance of a document, has the same effect as delivery of an executed original of this Agreement.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, THE PARTIES HAVE EXECUTED THIS AGREEMENT AS OF THE EFFECTIVE DATE ABOVE
